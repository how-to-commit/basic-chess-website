The Scotch Game

The Scotch Game is an opening characterised by the move d4, in an attempt to threaten black's e5 pawn. This move is extremely aggressive (one of the most aggressive options in the Open Game!) and often results in an open centre.

  1. e4 e5
  2. Nf3 Nc6
  3. d4

After 3. d4, the best move for black is exd4, though Nxd4 is also playable.

White will almost always play Nxd4 to take the pawn back, but this can transition into gambit lines with 4. Bc4 or 4. c3, to attempt to develop quickly.

  1. d4 exd4
  2. Nxd4

4...Bc5

Bc5 immediately attacks the knight on d4 and builds pressure on the weak f2 pawn, which black can threaten in the future with Qf6.

White's move here is Be3, defending the knight and the f2 pawn while also threatening to win a piece with Nxc6 dxc6 (if black responds with Bxe3, Nxd8 wins the black queen) Bxc5.

  1. Nxd4 Bc5
  2. Be3

4...Nf6

Nf6 immediately attacks white's central control by attacking white's central pawn.

  1. Nxd4 Nf6
