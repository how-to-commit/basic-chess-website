The Sodium Attack

The Sodium Attack ("Sodium", as the algebraic notation of the first move is Na3, Na being the chemical symbol for sodium), sometimes called the Dirkin Opening, is rarely played. It does naught to enhance white's position, though some sources consider it to be better than its reputation.

The Sodium Attack generally follows this move order.

  1. Na3

After 1. Na3, black's best option generally is 1...d5 as white's queenside knight cannot threaten d5 while being on a3, though other moves like 1...e5 and 1...g6 (to fianchetto the dark square bishop) are viable.

1...d5

  1. Na3 d5
  2. c4

After 1...d5, world champion Magnus Carlsen has made 2. c4 a viable option for white, immediately threatening black's central control with a pawn defended by the knight.
