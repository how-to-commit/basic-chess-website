The Scandinavian Defense

The goal of this defense is to force white into an open game, by directly threatening white's e4 pawn. This immediately breaks the center open and forces white to respond immediately.

The Scandinavian Defense's Mainline generally follows this move order:

  1. e4 d5
  2. exd5 Qxd5
  3. Nc3

After this set of moves, the opening diverges into a few distinct lines.

3...Qa5 (The Classical Scandinavian)

3...Qa5 is the most popular option. This threatens to pin white's knight to his king if white were to advance the d pawn. Furthermore, it allows black to develop his pieces, though black should be aware that his queen might be in danger of being trapped, and c6 should be played to avoid that.

3...Qd8

3...Qd8 is the next most popular line, allowing for black to retreat his queen to safety. However, black becomes extremely passive after this move and white quickly develops, taking central control with d4 or Bf4. This line is considered to be playable, but worse than 3...Qa5.
